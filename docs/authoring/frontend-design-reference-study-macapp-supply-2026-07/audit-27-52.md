## Cross-patterns

- The strongest pages pair a signature carrier with a proof carrier; they are often different assets. Dia/SlapMac supply identity, while Framer/Droppy/Glaze supply inspectable behavior.
- Product proof in or immediately after the first viewport is the most reusable success pattern. Logos, badges, gradients, and atmosphere do not substitute for it.
- Mobile quality depends on preserving the proof role, not the same layout. Framer, Superlist, AutoShelf, and Klack deliberately swap/crop/recompose assets.
- Most visible failures are state failures rather than taste failures: consent overlays, black/empty media, missing mobile artifacts, and product proof sliding below the fold.
- Long feature catalogs repeatedly dilute otherwise good openings (Fantastical, Noticky, Picmal, Creavit, Grape, likely Glyph). Section count should be a review signal, not a target.
- Semantic debt clusters in visually ambitious microsites (SlapMac, Bauhaus Clock) and older/catalog-heavy pages (Fantastical, Codeshot). Taste review should include landmarks, heading hierarchy, alt text, and media fallback.
- “Centered headline + gradient word + dual CTA + bento grid” is the dominant generic failure mode. A real object, native UI crop, or inspectable interaction is the most reliable antidote.
- Proposed skill-level implication: require explicit signature carrier, proof carrier, first-proof position, mobile proof transformation, motion role, state/fallback QA, section-budget rationale, and generic-pattern flags before approving a brief.
