## Cross-patterns

1. **The highest-leverage discriminator is proof position.** Strong pages put a product artifact inside the hero or touching the first viewport (LookAway, Obsidian, OpenSS, Open Timer, Corner Time, Compresto). Atlas proves the failure mode: desktop proof disappears entirely on mobile.
2. **Mobile should preserve the evidence contract, not the desktop composition.** OpenSS/Open Timer reorder proof above copy; Compresto changes conversion mechanics; Hejour drops marginalia but keeps demos. Alcove, Atlas, Liqoria, Cooldock, Supaste show why shrinking/hiding alone is insufficient.
3. **Best signatures are product-relative.** HeatScope’s heatmap, Compresto’s numeric sliders, Pokey’s click interaction, Cooldock/Supaste’s Mac-notch scene, and Obsidian’s thought graph communicate the product. Generic glow, badge, icon, and centered headline do not.
4. **Motion earns its cost in three roles:** direct demonstration (Corner Time, LookAway), spatial/system explanation (VoiceOS, Obsidian), or one memorable brand beat (Hejour). Shimmer/ambient blobs without an explanatory role should be treated as optional polish.
5. **IA works best when the product supplies the taxonomy.** Unfold organizes by file types; Room Service by developer workflows; Dropzone by changed behaviors. Repeating an alternating screenshot/text template without a product-derived sequence becomes monotonous.
6. **One-screen microsites are viable for tiny jobs** when the simulated state is self-explanatory (OpenSS/Open Timer). They fail when they remove the only proof (Atlas) or give no recovery depth.
7. **Trust mode varies by product.** OSS utilities use GitHub/stars; local/private tools use device/privacy details; measurable tools should use exact deltas; broad apps need dashboard orientation. A universal “social-proof strip” would flatten these distinctions.
8. **Skill implication:** add explicit contracts for archetype selection, first-viewport proof, mobile proof preservation, signature/product relationship, motion role, and semantic/z-index/overflow QA. Keep visual styles and section templates as references, not universal rules.
