## Cross-patterns

1. **Product truth dominates the first fold.** The strongest pages show actual UI, a demo, generated output, or the native Mac environment before a feature-card grid. CleanShot, GatherOS, Onlook, Runey, Wallspace, Keeby, and Backdrop are the clearest examples.
2. **Proof has three useful forms.** Mature products use quantified trust + logos (Notion, CleanMyMac); indie products use a launch badge or a named quote (DeskMinder, Screen Studio, CleanShot); visual/sensory tools make the artifact itself the proof (mymind, Waterlemon, GatherOS, Keeby).
3. **Proof proximity matters more than proof volume.** Screen Charm, CleanShot, Notion, Screen Studio, and Backdrop place one high-signal proof unit within or immediately after the hero. Pages that defer testimonials until deep below depend much more heavily on product imagery.
4. **Motion is strongest when it performs one of three jobs:** demonstrate output, simulate native context, or communicate product state. Motion used only as atmospheric glow adds cost without much evidence.
5. **Mobile needs art direction, not stacking.** Most sites preserve the product artifact and primary claim. Notion promotes quantified trust; Subscription Day swaps desktop screens for phone screens. DynamicLake is the sharp negative case: most media and proof disappear.
6. **A single brand world beats component variety.** Paper in Craft, macOS desktop in Onlook, wallpaper/lock screen in Wallspace and Backdrop, and the orange tactile desktop in Keeby remain coherent across many sections without inventing new card styles.
7. **Semantic and first-paint resilience are weak spots.** Several sites use one giant wrapper, no H1, canvas/video-heavy heroes, or entrance effects that can leave a blank/dark initial capture. Essential proposition and CTA should remain readable before animation succeeds.
8. **Reference state must be frozen.** Notion and Cursor prove that cookies, authentication, referral routing, region, and overlays can produce a different artifact from the intended public marketing page. A design-reference packet needs canonical URL, anonymous/session state, region, viewport, and overlay policy.

## Implications to test against `frontend-design`

- Add an explicit **proof-topology decision**: artifact proof, quantified trust, named authority, ecosystem compatibility, or community scale—and require a reason for its position.
- Add a **mobile proof-retention gate**, not merely mobile layout coverage.
- Expand motion critique from “present/reduced-motion-safe” to **role: demo / context / state / ambience**, rejecting ambience that steals the evidence budget.
- Add **first-paint resilience** to rendered QA: proposition, CTA, and fallback image must survive before canvas/video/entrance animation completes.
- Freeze **reference-state metadata** for external inspiration captures so authenticated or referral states cannot silently contaminate comparison.
- Keep the existing anti-generic doctrine: these pages are strongest when one brand-relative material or environment organizes the whole composition, not when they simply use prettier cards.
