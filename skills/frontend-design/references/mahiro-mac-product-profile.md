# Mahiro Mac Product Aesthetic Profile

Use this profile as an owner-approved **strong positive prior** for a confirmed Mahiro personal project when explicit product, brand, and repo direction are too thin to produce a material presentation or working-surface design decision.

This is a personal preference, not repo-proven reality, a corpus-derived truth, or universal design doctrine. Explicit user/product requirements, repo evidence, established components, accessibility behavior, and approved references override it.

## 1. Activation Contract

Activate only when all are true:

- ownership is confirmed as a Mahiro personal project;
- `frontend-design` is already active for a material visual-direction decision;
- explicit product/brand requirements and repo evidence are absent, thin, or insufficient for that decision;
- no approved reference or established design system supplies a stronger direction.

Ownership is confirmed when Mahiro explicitly identifies the work as personal, the current request explicitly selects this profile, or repo-local docs/trusted project memory explicitly label it as Mahiro's personal project. A repository namespace, package author, remote owner, or local path alone is not proof of personal scope. If ownership is the only material uncertainty, ask one bundled question instead of silently reverting to generic judgment.

An explicit request for this profile may activate it directly, but the same evidence precedence still applies.

Do not activate it:

- for client, employer, team, third-party, or uncertain-ownership work;
- merely because a product runs on or targets macOS;
- for routine implementation, bug fixes, or a native model-taste baseline;
- when it conflicts with an established brand, product behavior, accessibility contract, or repo design system.

Record activation as **owner-approved direction**, list the evidence gap it fills, and keep each recommendation reversible. If stronger evidence appears, remove or translate the fallback rather than defending it.

## 2. Shared Character

Aim for a product that feels:

- precise without becoming sterile;
- tactile without becoming ornamental;
- focused without feeling empty;
- compact where work benefits from density, calm where reading or creation needs space;
- technically credible without control-room theater;
- recognizably its own product without imitating operating-system chrome or another application.

Use these positive relationships when compatible with the product:

- a quiet, low-noise canvas with a small number of clearly differentiated surface tiers;
- crisp alignment, restrained chrome, hairline separation, and elevation reserved for real depth or transient context;
- disciplined typography with confident role contrast, concise labels, and practical metadata;
- one controlled accent family rather than unrelated gradients and decorative status colors;
- visually compact controls with usable hit targets and unmistakable interaction states;
- short, low-amplitude motion that explains state, location, direct manipulation, or causality;
- one product-derived signature relationship that shapes composition, interaction, or proof.

Construct hierarchy and tactility visibly:

- attach controls to the object, edge, pane, or selection they modify;
- show pane ownership through adjacency, headers, tonal tiers, and persistent selection—not cards around every region;
- keep contextual actions near the current object while global commands stay stable and quiet;
- anchor menus, inspectors, previews, and transient layers to the trigger or object that caused them;
- make direct manipulation produce an immediate, visible consequence with an honest cancel, undo, or recovery path;
- let local selection emphasis dominate its neighborhood without turning the whole canvas into an accent field.

Before choosing a composition, run this derivation:

1. Name the primary object the user sees or manipulates.
2. Name its most characteristic transformation, state change, environment, or result.
3. Make that relationship determine the dominant composition or workspace geometry.
4. Name the generic composition rejected because it would weaken that relationship.

A written signature claim without this visible derivation is not enough.

Do not treat minimalism as the goal. The goal is **resolved product character**: the primary object, action, evidence, and current state should feel deliberately composed.

## 3. Presentation-Surface Profile

Use this lane for acquisition, product explanation, editorial or documentation landing surfaces, pricing, and other presentation jobs. A browser-based editor, dashboard, configuration surface, or repeated workflow belongs to the working-surface lane instead. Mixed products may use both lanes per route or surface.

### Composition and hierarchy

- Derive the opening composition from the product's defining behavior, object, environment, or measurable result.
- Connect proposition, primary action, and meaningful product proof in or near the first viewport when seeing the product is necessary for belief.
- Use generous space to clarify hierarchy, not to postpone evidence.
- Let section order follow workflow, object model, comparison logic, trust requirements, or acquisition decisions.
- Prefer fewer, stronger chapters over repeated feature cards or interchangeable screenshot-copy bands.
- Keep navigation and conversion controls restrained; one dominant reading path should survive without decorative copy.

Choose a product-derived composition family only when the evidence supports it, or invent an equivalent relationship:

- **Object → transformation → result** when a file, capture, document, media object, or measurable delta carries the promise.
- **Behavior demonstration** when one real interaction explains the product better than a static feature list.
- **Environment / compatibility proof** when the product earns belief by showing where it works, what it connects to, or how it fits an existing workflow.

These are decision families, not reusable hero templates. Their geometry, sequence, crop, and interaction must come from the actual product object and claim.

### Typography and product media

- Use deliberate role contrast and line breaks. Do not use giant type to compensate for weak proof.
- Preserve the repo's established type system; do not force a platform font merely to signal Mac.
- Keep product UI, documents, media, before/after evidence, and native context inspectable rather than using them as background texture.
- Window, menu, dock, wallpaper, device, or translucent framing must explain where or how the product works.

### Material, motion, and trust

- Prefer restrained neutral surfaces and one intentional accent when compatible with the brand; avoid glass, glow, gradients, pills, and floating shells as automatic platform shorthand.
- Give motion one declared role: demonstrate behavior, explain spatial/system context, or deliver one restrained brand beat.
- Keep proposition, proof, and action understandable before animation, under reduced motion, and when media fails.
- Match trust proof to the real uncertainty: inspectable UI, measurable result, compatibility, privacy/local processing, technical detail, provenance, pricing, or acquisition clarity.

### Responsive quality

Mobile preserves the **proof role**, not the desktop arrangement. Reorder, crop, replace, or simplify the artifact when needed. Do not hide the only meaningful evidence, shrink it into texture, or preserve desktop typography mechanically.

## 4. Working-Surface Profile

Use this lane for repeated tasks, editing, creation, configuration, monitoring, management, and review regardless of whether the runtime is native, desktop-web, or browser-based.

### Workspace composition

- Organize the interface around the primary object and repeated task.
- Keep the working area dominant, with stable navigation, inspectable content, contextual controls, and visible consequences.
- Use sidebars, lists, inspectors, toolbars, menus, split views, popovers, and shortcuts only when the information architecture earns them.
- Avoid landing-page heroes, decorative metric grids, cards for every group, atmospheric empty space, and explanatory marketing copy inside working surfaces.

Choose a workspace relationship only when the task model earns it, or derive a better one:

- **List → workspace → inspector** for selecting many objects, acting on one, and examining contextual detail.
- **Focused canvas + contextual tools** for creation or manipulation where the object must stay visually dominant.
- **Source → transformation → result** for workflows where causality, preview, and comparison must remain visible.

Do not force three panes or permanent sidebars. Collapse, merge, or replace regions when the product's object count, task frequency, or window size does not justify them.

### Typography and density

- Favor compact, readable hierarchy for sustained use; let spacing, alignment, contrast, and grouping establish priority before type size.
- Use denser layouts for scanning, comparison, and manipulation, and calmer rhythm for creation, reading, and focused review.
- Treat metadata as useful information, not tiny low-contrast decoration.
- Keep controls visually compact while preserving practical pointer and touch targets.

### Material and components

- Surfaces must clarify ownership, selection, depth, and context.
- Use border shifts, background tiers, translucency, shadow, and layering sparingly and consistently.
- Reserve strong elevation for overlays, menus, transient feedback, or genuine hierarchy.
- Make selection, focus, hover, pressed, disabled, loading, drag, destructive, and validation states distinct.
- Preserve selection and state meaning without relying on color alone; keep focus, separators, metadata, and disabled treatment legible across every surface tier.
- Use clear, consistent icons with labels where the action is not universally recognizable.
- Do not cosmetically recreate native chrome when repo primitives communicate the behavior more honestly or accessibly.

### Motion, state, and adaptation

- Motion explains context opening, selection, navigation, reordering, drag-and-drop, result causality, or state transition.
- Avoid routine hover scaling, spring choreography, and ambient motion in working surfaces.
- Keyboard access, focus return, cancellation, undo, menu behavior, and reduced motion are part of the visual quality.
- Empty, first-run, loading, error, offline, permission, unavailable, and destructive states must retain the same product character.
- At narrow widths or compact windows, preserve the task and object relationship instead of stacking every pane. Collapse secondary context deliberately and provide a clear recovery path.
- Verify text zoom and scaling against compact geometry, and keep native-looking controls semantically named with the correct interactive role.

## 5. Thai and Localization

- Treat Thai as a first-class content shape, not a final substitution.
- Expect different line breaks, taller texture, longer labels, and mixed Thai/Latin baselines.
- Avoid fixed-height text containers, forced uppercase, excessive letter spacing, and tiny muted labels.
- Keep technical terms in English when clearer, but make surrounding Thai direct and natural.
- Test menu width, toolbar crowding, truncation, sorting, search, dates, numbers, long filenames, metadata, and realistic content stress.
- Marketing line breaks may be art-directed per locale, but must not depend on English word length to preserve the composition.

## 6. Anti-Cloning Boundaries

This profile does not authorize:

- copying Apple or third-party hero anatomy, window arrangements, icons, wallpaper, typography, copy voice, or motion sequences;
- ornamental traffic lights, fake titlebars, generic desktop scenes, or device shells used as product proof;
- white space, translucency, rounded rectangles, monochrome palettes, or oversized app icons as a complete identity;
- the same centered icon hero, split screenshot, simulated desktop, cinematic dark stage, or floating glass shell across unrelated products;
- mandated fonts, palettes, radii, section counts, frameworks, component systems, symbol libraries, or motion engines;
- invented native behavior, platform capability, or accessibility semantics;
- replacing truthful product evidence with atmosphere.

A result should still belong to the product after removing its logo. If swapping only color, type, and imagery can turn it into an unrelated product, the signature is too generic.

## 7. Acceptance Checks

- **Activation** — Is ownership confirmed, direction materially thin, and the fallback explicitly applicable?
- **Override** — Did stronger user, product, brand, repo, accessibility, or reference evidence replace conflicting fallback choices?
- **Lane integrity** — Does a presentation surface prioritize proposition and proof while a working surface prioritizes repeated task and state, regardless of browser/native runtime?
- **Product derivation** — Does the main relationship come from real behavior, objects, workflow, environment, results, or trust needs?
- **Signature** — Does one recognizable relationship shape composition or interaction rather than act as decoration?
- **Proof** — Is each material claim connected to inspectable, appropriate evidence?
- **Hierarchy** — Are the primary object, action, and next step clear without atmospheric filler?
- **Material** — Does every layer, blur, shadow, frame, and platform cue clarify context?
- **Density** — Does rhythm fit the work instead of performing generic premium minimalism?
- **Motion** — Does motion have one declared job and a static/reduced-motion path?
- **Responsive/adaptive** — Does every target viewport preserve the task or proof role?
- **States** — Are first paint, loading, empty, error, disabled, focus, overlays, overflow, and media failure resolved?
- **Visual accessibility** — Do focus, selection, separators, metadata, and status survive low-contrast surface tiers, non-color use, and text zoom?
- **Localization** — Does realistic Thai and mixed-language content remain readable and structurally sound?
- **Originality** — Is the result still recognizably product-specific without its logo?
